1. "Just stop using this ADHD thing as an excuse."
    - Ableist: 8
        - This statement is highly ableist because it dismisses ADHD as an "excuse," implying that the person with ADHD is intentionally avoiding responsibility or using their condition as a cop-out. It perpetuates harmful stereotypes about mental health conditions.
    - Toxic: 6
        - While not extremely toxic, this statement can still hurt. It invalidates the challenges faced by someone with ADHD and may cause emotional distress.
    - Reasons:
        - Minimization: The phrase "just stop" minimizes the impact of ADHD, implying that it's a minor issue that can be easily overcome.
        - Blame and Judgment: Labeling ADHD as an "excuse" places blame on the individual, suggesting they're intentionally avoiding responsibilities.
        - Lack of Empathy: The lack of empathy in this statement contributes to its toxicity.
